# Drug–Adverse Reaction Causality Assessment

*(Based on WHO-UMC style causality categories: Certain / Probable / Possible)*

---

## A. Case Study Summary

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| **Patient** | 45-year-old Female |
| **Suspected Drug** | Amoxicillin |
| **Adverse Reaction** | Skin Rash |
| **Onset** | 3 days after starting the drug |
| **Outcome** | Recovered after stopping the drug |

---

## B. Assessment Criteria

| # | Criterion | Question | Finding | Assessment |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 01 | **Temporal Relationship** | Did the reaction occur after the drug was administered? | Yes — rash appeared 3 days after starting Amoxicillin | Positive / Plausible |
| 02 | **Dechallenge** | Did the reaction improve after stopping the drug? | Yes — patient recovered after the drug was withdrawn | Positive |
| 03 | **Alternative Causes** | Could the reaction be explained by another cause? | No alternative cause (other illness, other drug, food, etc.) is reported in the case | Unlikely to be explained otherwise |
| 04 | **Rechallenge (if applicable)** | Did the reaction reappear on re-administration? | Not done / not reported | Not assessable |

---

## C. Causality Classification

| Category | Criteria Required | Met in this case? |
|---|---|---|
| **Certain** | Plausible time relationship + positive dechallenge + **positive rechallenge** + no alternative explanation | ❌ No — rechallenge was not performed, so "Certain" cannot be assigned |
| **Probable** | Plausible time relationship + positive dechallenge + no alternative explanation, **rechallenge not required** | ✅ Yes — all conditions satisfied |
| **Possible** | Plausible time relationship, but dechallenge unclear/not done, or alternative causes cannot be ruled out | ❌ Does not fit — dechallenge is clear and positive here |

### ✅ Classification: **Probable**

---

### Justification
This case shows a **clear temporal relationship** (rash within 3 days of starting Amoxicillin), a **positive dechallenge** (recovery after stopping the drug), and **no identified alternative cause** for the reaction. However, since **rechallenge was not performed**, the highest level of certainty ("Certain") cannot be confirmed — that classification is reserved for cases where re-exposure to the drug reproduces the reaction. With three of the four criteria clearly positive and rechallenge simply not applicable/not done, this reaction is best classified as **Probable**.
